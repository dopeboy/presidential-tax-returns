Presidents talk a lot of about taxes. Let's find out how much they actually pay.

Since the early 1970s, presidents and presidental candidates have voluntarily released their tax returns in order to provide transparency. Tax returns help answer questions like:

Do you pay taxes? Do you give to charity? Do you use tax loopholes? Do you keep your money offshore? Can you properly manage your family's finances? Are you financially beholden to other people or entities?

We hope the data elow will give you insight into these questions across past presidents and candidates so that you can better evaluate current and future candidates.

Insights

The president or candidate with the most data is Hillary and Bill Clinton, with 24 years of tax returns. Barack and Michelle Obama come in second with 16 years of returns.
Presidents or candidates making less than $250,000: Democrats give twice as much to charity and pay more 50% taxes than Republicans
Presidents or candidates making between $250,000 and $1,000,000: Democrats give 50% more to charity, but pay less in effective taxes. The combined charity + taxes is higher for Republicans.
Presidents or candidates making more than $1,000,000: Democrats give more to charity and pay higher effective taxes, with a combined total of 42% for Democrats and 38% for Republicans.
The president or candidate with the most data is Hillary and Bill Clinton, with 24 years of tax returns. Barack and Michelle Obama come in second with 16 years of returns.
The highest charity rate in a given year is George and Barbara Bush at 62%. Hillary and Bill Clinton come in second, at 56%.
Hillary and Bill Clinton have the highest total income ($244m), as well as the highest amount give to charity ($24m) and taxes paid ($77m).
Unlike his father (40%) and brother (9.35%), Jeb Bush gave very little of his $24m in income to charity (1.49%).
